Childhood Games to College Goals

Quick Navigation Childhood Years Teenage Years College Life

Childhood Years

I was born and raised in a family filled with love and life lessons, and I am originally from Tandang Sora, Quezon City. In my daily life as a child, I was always full of joy and happiness because during those times, I had no responsibilities to think about or carry. Back then, all I ever thought about was what games I would play throughout the day. I would often find ways to escape the house in the afternoon because I was forced to take naps, as my parents believed that sleeping in the afternoon would help me grow faster. But because I was stubborn and eager to play with my friends, I disobeyed my parents’ instructions to sleep, and I was often punished for it. I was also the kind of child who was always out in the streets because that was where I spent time with other kids who brought me happiness. In games like patintero, luksong baka, and tumbang preso, I was able to show my agility and my resourceful way of playing. My family taught me how to be cheerful and how to bring happiness to others. At a young age, I learned different ways to bring joy and make my family happy. And this was the time when I started realizing that I can do more things.

Teenage Years

My teenage years were full of challenges, changes, and self-reflection. It was during this time that I had deep realizations about myself and my plans for the future. I met many new friends who became my companions in life; they are an important part of my journey because they shared both the good and bad experiences with me. I learned to be more disciplined and to value the things I hold important in life—my time at school, with my family, my friends, and for myself. When I was in elementary school, I prioritized playing over studying. But in high school, I focused more on my studies because I saw my potential to become an academic achiever. And because of my hard work in school, from Grade 9 to Grade 12, I received the “With High Honors” award consistently. I am also proud to have graduated from Senior High School as the Top 1 of our strand. My teenage years taught me to become a better person and to keep improving myself.

College Years

My current experience in college as a 2nd year Bachelor of Science in Information Technology student studying at the University of the East - Manila is one of my biggest stages in life. My responsibilities and time for studying increased because of the many activities, assignments, quizzes, and exams. In college, I also realized that I need to be more careful in choosing my circle of friends and who I should work with. Being a college student is extremely stressful because of the many deadlines I have to meet. There are times when I don’t get to sleep because I still need to finish activities with very short deadlines. I realized that I shouldn’t stay in my comfort zone and that I need to broaden my perspective in the things I do. Being a college student made me realize that I am truly growing up. I can also see myself more clearly on the path I am taking, which is becoming an IT specialist. I believe I can overcome all of that because I trust in my abilities and in God’s plan. Padayon!
